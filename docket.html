June Is National Homeownership Month

June is National Homeownership Month, and at the Sacramento Association of REALTORS®, it is a cause we champion every single day.

 

Homeownership is one of the most powerful tools available for building generational wealth, creating stability, and putting down roots in a community. Every family deserves the opportunity to experience that, and our members are on the front lines making it happen.

 

From first-time buyers navigating the process for the very first time to longtime homeowners protecting the equity they have built, Sacramento REALTORS® show up as trusted advisors through one of the most significant financial decisions a person will ever make.

 

This month, we celebrate the families who have achieved the dream of homeownership and recommit to the work of making it more attainable for everyone in our region.

Legislative Update

Court Allows NAR Amicus Brief in Challenge to NY Rent Stabilization Law

This week, a federal court granted NAR permission to file an amicus brief in a case challenging New York’s Rent Stabilization Law, a dispute that could have major implications for property rights and housing affordability. If rent-control-style restrictions are upheld or expanded in a way that limits vacancy rent increases, the result can be less reinvestment, fewer available units, and worsening supply pressures.

 

That’s precisely why contributing to the REALTORS® Action Fund (RAF) matters—so champions at the ballot box can push for policies that support housing availability and protect the ability of responsible owners to maintain and invest in housing.

Regional Meetings & Tours


Regional meetings, formerly MLS meetings, are weekly gatherings of SAR Members and Preferred Partners from various agencies and brokerages. They offer opportunities to network with fellow REALTORS®, present listings, receive finance updates, and tour new homes on the market. Meetings are held at seven locations: Arden / Carmichael, Downtown / Midtown, Elk Grove, Folsom, Natomas, Northeast, and West Sacramento—typically on the 2nd and 4th Tuesdays of the month.
