Crisis communications

Long Island Divided

In 2019, Newsday published “Long Island Divided,” a three-year investigation into housing discrimination across Long Island's real estate industry. I was on the communications team at the Long Island Board of REALTORS® through the difficult public reckoning that followed.

It remains the clearest lesson of my career in what high-stakes communication actually asks of you: accuracy over speed, accountability over spin, and steady, honest messaging to members and the public at a moment when saying nothing would have been the easier path.

That experience is a large part of why I think about a communications role the way your posting describes it: the right message, to the right audience, at the right time, told in a voice people can trust.
